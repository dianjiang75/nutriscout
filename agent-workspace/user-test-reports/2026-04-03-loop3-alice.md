## Detailed Notes

### Tests 1-5: Diet Keyword Filtering

No violations found. Every dish returned under each diet filter was scanned for prohibited keywords in both `name` and `description` fields.

- **Vegan (test 4):** 10 dishes returned. None contained chicken, beef, pork, lamb, fish, egg, cheese, cream, or eel.
- **Vegetarian (test 5):** 26 dishes returned. None contained chicken, beef, pork, lamb, fish, or eel in name.
- **Gluten-free (test 2):** 35 dishes returned. No sourdough, toast, bread, or pasta in name or description.
- **Dairy-free (test 3):** 33 dishes returned. No feta, pecorino, cheese, cream, or butter in name or description.
- **Nut-free + Gluten-free (test 1):** 34 dishes returned. No udon, ramen, noodle, pizza, peanut, or almond in name.

### Tests 6-7: Query + Diet Exclusion

- **Pad Thai + nut_free:** 0 results. Pad thai (typically contains peanuts) correctly excluded.
- **Udon + gluten_free:** 0 results. Udon (wheat noodle) correctly excluded.

### Tests 8-9: Allergen vs Diet Cross-Check

- **Test 8 (nuts):** `allergens=tree_nuts,peanuts` returned 35 dishes; `diet=nut_free` returned 36. One dish ("Pork Belly Tostada") appeared only in the diet filter. Minor discrepancy -- likely due to different filtering logic (allergen tags vs dietary boolean flag). Not a safety violation since the dish itself is nut-free.
- **Test 9 (gluten):** `allergens=wheat,gluten` returned 20 dishes; `diet=gluten_free` returned 35. The diet filter is broader (returns more results). 15 dishes appeared only in `diet=gluten_free`, including sushi items and fried dishes. This is expected: the `allergens` filter checks explicit allergen tags, while the `diet` flag is a higher-level boolean. No gluten-containing dishes leaked into the gluten_free results.

### Test 10: Triple Diet Filter

29 dishes returned. Every dish confirmed `nut_free=true`, `gluten_free=true`, `dairy_free=true` in `dietary_flags`.
